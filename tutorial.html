How to Use Free Time

A simple guide to getting started with mindful phone usage

Getting Started

1. Download and Install

Download Free Time from the App Store and open the app on your iPhone or iPad.

Requirements: iOS 16.0 or later

2. Grant Screen Time Permissions

When you first open Free Time, you'll be asked to grant Screen Time permissions. This is essential for the app to work.

  1. Tap "Get Started" in the app
  2. When prompted, tap "Allow" to grant Screen Time access
  3. Follow the system prompts to enable permissions in Settings

Note: Free Time uses Apple's official Screen Time API. Your data stays private and on your device.

3. Select Apps to Block

Choose which apps you want to manage with Free Time.

  1. Go to the Apps tab
  2. Tap "Add Apps" or "Manage Apps"
  3. Select the apps you want to block from the list
  4. Organize apps into groups (like "Social Media" or "Games") for easier management

Tip: Start with your most distracting apps - you can always add more later.

Setting Up Blocking

4. Create a Blocking Schedule

Set up when you want apps to be blocked automatically.

  1. Go to the Schedule tab
  2. Tap "Create New Schedule"
  3. Choose which days of the week (e.g., weekdays only)
  4. Set start and end times (e.g., 9 AM to 5 PM for work hours)
  5. Select which app groups to block during this time
  6. Save your schedule

Examples:

  • Focus Hours: Block social media Monday-Friday, 9 AM - 5 PM
  • Evening Wind-Down: Block all apps daily, 10 PM - 7 AM
  • Always-On: Block certain apps 24/7 by selecting all days

5. Use Instant Focus (Optional)

Need to block apps right now? Use Instant Focus mode.

  1. Go to the Focus tab
  2. Choose a preset duration (15 min, 30 min, 1 hour, etc.)
  3. Tap "Start Focus"

Your selected apps will be blocked immediately for the chosen duration.

Using the App Daily

6. Understanding Puzzles

When you try to open a blocked app, you'll need to complete a puzzle or mindfulness activity to unlock it temporarily.

Puzzle Types:

  • Math Puzzles: Solve arithmetic problems. Choose Easy, Medium, or Hard difficulty in Settings.
  • Box Breathing: Complete a calming breathing exercise (inhale-hold-exhale-hold).
  • Gratitude Journal: Type something you're grateful for to shift your mindset before using the app.

What happens after solving a puzzle?
The blocked app unlocks for a set time period (you can customize this in Settings). After the time expires, the app blocks again.

7. Customize Your Settings

Make Free Time work for you by adjusting settings:

  • Puzzle Difficulty: Change how challenging puzzles are
  • Unlock Duration: Set how long apps stay unlocked after completing a puzzle
  • Puzzle Types: Enable or disable specific puzzle types
  • Notifications: Get reminders and progress updates

Access all settings from the Settings tab.

Tips for Success

Best Practices

  • Start Small: Begin by blocking just 1-2 apps or creating one simple schedule. Build up gradually.
  • Be Realistic: Don't block apps you truly need for work or important communication.
  • Use App Groups: Organize apps by category (Social Media, Games, Shopping) for easier management.
  • Adjust as Needed: If puzzles feel too easy or too hard, change the difficulty. Free Time should help, not frustrate.
  • Review Regularly: Check your usage patterns weekly and adjust schedules accordingly.
  • Combine Methods: Use both scheduled blocking and Instant Focus for maximum flexibility.

Common Questions

What if I need to access a blocked app urgently?

You can always complete a puzzle to unlock the app temporarily. For true emergencies, you can disable Free Time permissions in iPhone Settings → Screen Time → Free Time.

Can I bypass the puzzles?

No - that's the point! The puzzles create a mindful pause between impulse and action. However, you're always in control and can adjust settings or disable blocking if needed.

Will this drain my battery?

No. Free Time uses Apple's native Screen Time framework, which is designed to be energy-efficient and runs in the background with minimal impact.
