Best Digital Detox Retreats in Italy (Phone-Free & Tech-Free, Tuscany)

Italy's most established meditation and retreat centers are concentrated in Tuscany — specifically in the Province of Pisa, in a stretch of hills between the Etruscan city of Volterra and the coast south of Pisa. This is not the Tuscany of wine tours and agriturismo — it is quieter, more interior, and more remote. Two centers anchor the landscape: Osho Miasto, an international Osho meditation community, and Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa (ILTK), one of Europe's most respected Tibetan Buddhist institutes.

For a digital detox, what both centers share is a setting — Tuscan hills, cypress trees, olive groves, relative isolation from cities — and a culture that makes device use feel inappropriate. Whether from an Osho meditation perspective or a Tibetan Buddhist practice perspective, the point of being there is to be present, not online.

Digital Detox Retreats in Italy

Osho Miasto

Pomarance, Province of Pisa, Tuscany Screen-minimal culture Osho / Active meditation International programs in English ~1.5 hours from Pisa, ~2 hours from Florence

Osho Miasto ("Osho City" in Italian) is an Osho meditation community and retreat center in Pomarance, in the Tuscan hills of the Province of Pisa — the same area as Volterra, the ancient Etruscan hilltop city. The center is part of the international network of Osho centers that grew from Osho Rajneesh's teachings and is one of the more active Osho communities in Europe.

The center offers residential programs in Osho's distinctive approach to meditation — which includes both traditional silent sitting and active meditation methods (Dynamic Meditation, Kundalini Meditation, No-Mind Meditation), body-based therapies, and expressive arts. International programs are conducted in English. The community culture is deeply immersive: life at the center revolves around meditation, daily practice, and community meals, leaving very little space for or desire to use a screen.

The Tuscan setting — rolling hills, cypress trees, the volcanic landscape of the metalliferous hills (Colline Metallifere) that distinguishes this part of Tuscany — is beautifully remote. Volterra is about 20 km away; the nearest coastal town is about 40 km.

Getting there: Fly to Pisa (PSA) and rent a car. From Pisa: approximately 80 km southeast via the SS439 through Volterra direction, about 1.5 hours. From Florence: approximately 100 km via the A1 and SS439/SS68, about 2 hours. A car is essential — the center is in rural Tuscany. Check oshomiasto.it for current programs and directions.

Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa (ILTK)

Pomaia, near Santa Luce, Province of Pisa, Tuscany Phone-free during retreat periods Tibetan Buddhist (FPMT / Gelug) ~1 hour from Pisa Airport Resident study programs + shorter retreats

Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa, known as ILTK, is one of the most respected Tibetan Buddhist study and practice centers in Europe. Founded in 1977 and part of FPMT (Foundation for the Preservation of the Mahayana Tradition), it is located in Pomaia, near Santa Luce and Chianni, in the Tuscan hills south of Pisa. The setting is a converted farmhouse complex surrounded by Tuscan countryside — cypress trees, olive groves, and quiet hills.

ILTK offers a full range of programs from beginner introductions to Buddhist philosophy through long-term residential study programs (some lasting multiple years). The program roster includes meditation courses, lam rim (graduated path) study, debate practice, retreats with visiting teachers from the Tibetan Buddhist world, and personal retreat stays. During all retreat periods, phones are not used. The center has a resident monastic community and lay practitioners.

For digital detox purposes, ILTK is one of the most thoroughly phone-free environments in Italy — the philosophy of practice makes device use during retreat periods simply inconsistent with the purpose of being there. Even during non-retreat study periods, the culture is profoundly non-digital.

Getting there: Fly to Pisa (PSA) and rent a car. From Pisa Airport: approximately 50-60 km south via the SS1 coastal highway and then inland via the SP15 and local roads — about 1 hour. From Florence: approximately 120 km via the A1 and SS1, about 1.5 hours. Check iltk.org for current programs, directions, and booking.

Cost: Varies by program length and type. Shorter retreat weekends start around €100-200. Week-long programs typically €400-700 including accommodation and meals. Residential study programs have different structures. Bursaries available for those in need.

Visit iltk.org →

Tuscany retreat tip: Osho Miasto and Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa are both in the Province of Pisa — about 30-40 km apart. If you're flying into Pisa for a retreat at one center, the other is easily reachable. The hill towns of Volterra and Massa Marittima are nearby if you want to spend a non-retreat day in the landscape before or after your program.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best digital detox retreats in Italy?

The two most established digital detox retreat centers in Italy are Osho Miasto (Pomarance, Province of Pisa, Tuscany — Osho meditation community, screen-minimal culture, international English programs) and Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa (Pomaia, Province of Pisa, Tuscany — FPMT Tibetan Buddhist, phone-free during retreats, one of Europe's most respected Buddhist institutes). Both are in the Tuscan hills, roughly 30-40 km apart, and accessible from Pisa Airport.

What is Osho Miasto?

Osho Miasto is an Osho meditation community in Pomarance, Tuscany (Province of Pisa). Part of the international Osho network, it offers residential meditation programs including both active and silent meditation methods, body-based therapies, and expressive arts workshops. International programs are in English. The Tuscan setting and community culture create a naturally screen-minimal environment during stays.

What is Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa?

ILTK is a Tibetan Buddhist study and practice center in Pomaia, Tuscany, part of FPMT (Foundation for the Preservation of the Mahayana Tradition). Founded in 1977, it offers everything from beginner meditation courses to multi-year residential Buddhist study programs. During retreat periods, phones are not used. It is considered one of the most serious Buddhist study centers in Europe and hosts visiting teachers from the Tibetan Buddhist world regularly.

How do I get to retreat centers in Tuscany from Florence or Pisa?

Both Osho Miasto and ILTK are in the Province of Pisa, in rural Tuscany. From Pisa Airport: Osho Miasto is ~80 km east (1.5 hours), ILTK is ~50 km south (1 hour). From Florence: approximately 2 hours by car to either center. A car is essential — both are in rural countryside without public transit access. Pisa Airport (PSA) is the most practical arrival point for either center.
