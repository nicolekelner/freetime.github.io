Best Digital Detox Retreats in Europe (Phone-Free & Tech-Free)

Europe has some of the world's most serious retreat centers — many of them established decades before smartphones existed, in landscapes that have drawn pilgrims and practitioners for centuries. The difference from the American retreat market is palpable: fewer luxury wellness formats, more grounded contemplative traditions. When Amaravati Monastery in Hertfordshire collects your phone at arrival, it's not a "digital detox program." It's just how a monastery has always operated.

These are places where the phone-free environment isn't a policy announcement — it's the natural consequence of the practice itself. Plum Village (France) draws practitioners from 90+ countries and has been observing Noble Silence since 1982. Gaia House (Devon) has no WiFi in retreat areas. Dzogchen Beara sits on an Atlantic clifftop in County Cork where the absence of signal feels like the point.

🇫🇷 France

France is home to Plum Village — Thich Nhat Hanh's landmark monastery in the Dordogne — and Centre Lerab Ling, Rigpa's main European teaching center in the hills of the Hérault. Both are in rural settings far from major cities, which reinforces the disconnection before you've put your phone away. See the France guide →

Plum Village

📍 Thenac, Dordogne (near Bordeaux) 🧘 Zen / Mindfulness Duration: 1–3 weeks
🚫 Phones not used during retreat periods — Noble Silence

The most internationally recognized Buddhist retreat center in Europe, founded in 1982 by Vietnamese Zen master Thich Nhat Hanh. Plum Village operates across three monasteries in the Dordogne and Gironde countryside near Bordeaux, receiving practitioners from over 90 countries each year.

Retreats are conducted in Noble Silence, with phones and devices not used during the program period. The practice emphasizes mindfulness in daily life — walking, eating, working. Major programs include a summer Opening in July, the Summer Retreat in August, and a Winter Retreat in December. Book 3–4 months in advance. Retreat weeks start around €500-700.

Visit plumvillage.org →

Centre Lerab Ling

📍 Roqueredonde, Hérault (near Montpellier) ☸️ Tibetan Buddhist (Rigpa) Duration: Weekend to 1 week+
🚫 Phone-free during all programs

Rigpa's main European retreat and teaching center, set in the limestone hills of the Hérault department in southern France, about 60 km northwest of Montpellier. The center has a large Tibetan shrine hall consecrated by the Dalai Lama, who has taught there on multiple occasions.

Programs range from short retreats to major annual teaching events. During all programs, phones and devices are not used. The remote Languedoc setting — farmland and hills — makes digital disconnection feel effortless.

Visit lerabling.org →

🇬🇧 England & Scotland

England has more internationally respected retreat centers per square mile than almost anywhere in Europe. Devon's concentration — Gaia House and Sharpham, 15 minutes apart near Totnes — is remarkable. Amaravati (Hertfordshire) is 40 miles from London, making it the most accessible serious silent retreat center from a major European capital. See the England & Scotland guide →

Amaravati Buddhist Monastery

📍 Great Gaddesden, Hertfordshire (~40 miles from London) 🔇 Thai Forest / Theravada Duration: 5–10 days
🚫 Phones collected at arrival — Noble Silence

A fully residential monastery in the Thai Forest tradition, founded in 1984 by Ajahn Sumedho. Retreats follow Noble Silence throughout, with phones collected at arrival and returned at the end. The daily schedule moves between sitting, walking, work practice, and Dhamma teachings.

One of the most beginner-welcoming silent retreat centers in England despite its serious monastic context. Offered on a dana (donation) basis — one of the most financially accessible retreat options in Europe. Reachable by train to Hemel Hempstead.

Visit amaravati.org →

Gaia House

📍 West Ogwell, Devon (~3 hours from London) 🔇 Insight Meditation / Vipassana Duration: 2 days to 1 month
🚫 Phones collected at retreat start — Noble Silence throughout

England's most established Western insight meditation center, in the countryside near Newton Abbot, Devon. Phones are collected at the start of every retreat and Noble Silence is maintained throughout. The lineage follows IMS (Barre, MA) — many teachers trained with Jack Kornfield, Sharon Salzberg, or Joseph Goldstein.

Retreats range from 2-day weekends to month-long programs. Many explicitly welcome beginners. About 3 hours from London by train to Newton Abbot.

Visit gaiahouse.co.uk →

Sharpham House & Sharpham Trust

📍 Ashprington, near Totnes, Devon 🧘 Mindfulness-based Duration: Weekend to 1 week
📵 Screens and devices strongly discouraged during retreats

An 18th-century Palladian manor on the banks of the River Dart near Totnes, run by the Sharpham Trust. Programs range from silent mindfulness intensives to creative and movement-based retreats. The Dart estuary setting — tidal water, woodland, estate farmland — creates a natural container for stepping away from digital life. Just 15-20 minutes from Gaia House.

Visit sharpham.com →

Samye Ling Tibetan Centre

📍 Eskdalemuir, Dumfries & Galloway, Scotland ☸️ Tibetan Buddhist (Kagyu) Duration: Weekend to extended
📵 Phone-free during retreat programs

The first Tibetan Buddhist center established in the Western world, founded in 1967 in the remote hills of Dumfries and Galloway, Scotland. Considered the largest Buddhist monastery in Western Europe, with a resident monastic community and an ornate Tibetan temple that feels incongruous and magnificent against the grey Scottish landscape.

Guest retreat accommodation is available for structured programs and personal retreat. During organized programs, phones and devices are not used. About 1.5 hours from Glasgow or Edinburgh by car.

Visit samyeling.org →

🇮🇪 Ireland

Ireland's most celebrated retreat center is on a clifftop on the Beara Peninsula in County Cork, overlooking the Atlantic. The combination of genuine Tibetan Buddhist practice and a landscape that has no need for a screen makes Dzogchen Beara singular. See the Ireland guide →

Dzogchen Beara

📍 Allihies, Beara Peninsula, County Cork ☸️ Tibetan Buddhist (Rigpa / Dzogchen) Duration: Weekend to 1 week+
🚫 Devices not used during retreat programs

Perched on the cliffs of the Beara Peninsula overlooking Coulagh Bay and the Atlantic, Dzogchen Beara is part of the Rigpa organization and one of the most strikingly located retreat centers in Europe. On clear days, the view extends to the Skellig Rocks — the ancient monastic islands that drew monks here for exactly the right reasons.

Programs include organized retreat weeks with resident and visiting teachers, plus personal retreat accommodation for independent retreat use. About 2.5 hours by car from Cork Airport via Bantry and Castletownbere.

Visit dzogchenbeara.org →

🇪🇸 Spain

Spain's most established retreat center — Cortijo Romero in the Alpujarras, which ran English-language holistic programs since 1986 — permanently closed in May 2024. The Spanish retreat scene now centers on Sakya Tashi Ling near Barcelona and Suryalila in Andalusia. See the Spain guide →

Sakya Tashi Ling

📍 Garraf Natural Park, near Barcelona, Catalonia ☸️ Tibetan Buddhist (Sakya) Duration: Weekend to extended
📵 Phone-free during retreat programs

A significant Tibetan Buddhist monastery complex in the Garraf Natural Park near Olivella, about 40 km southwest of Barcelona. The monastery features a large ornate shrine hall and stupa in Himalayan architectural style, striking in the Mediterranean limestone landscape.

Programs are offered in Catalan, Spanish, and English, ranging from meditation weekends to longer teachings and personal retreat accommodation. Accessible by car from Barcelona El Prat Airport in about 45 minutes.

Visit monjesbudistas.org →

Suryalila Retreat Center

📍 Cadiz province, Andalusia 🧘 Yoga / Wellness Duration: 1–3 weeks (incl. teacher trainings)
📵 Screen-minimal yoga retreat culture

A yoga retreat center in the hills of Cadiz province near the Sierra de Grazalema Natural Park in Andalusia. Known especially for Jivamukti and alignment-based yoga teacher trainings, Suryalila draws participants primarily from the UK, Germany, the Netherlands, and the US. All programs run in English.

The intensive yoga schedule leaves little space or desire for screens — and the Andalusian setting (olive groves, white villages, mountain landscape) reinforces the disconnection. Best reached by car from Seville or Jerez de la Frontera airports.

Visit suryalila.com →

🇮🇹 Italy

Italy's meditation retreat scene is concentrated in Tuscany — in the Province of Pisa, where two well-established international communities sit in the Tuscan hills 30-40 km apart, both accessible from Pisa Airport. See the Italy guide →

Osho Miasto

📍 Pomarance, near Volterra, Province of Pisa, Tuscany 🧘 Osho / Active Meditation Duration: Weekend to extended
📵 Screen-minimal during programs

An Osho meditation community in the Tuscan hills near the ancient Etruscan city of Volterra. The center offers Osho's distinctive approach to meditation — combining silent sitting and active meditation methods (Dynamic Meditation, Kundalini) with bodywork, expressive arts, and community life. International programs are conducted in English.

The Tuscan setting — volcanic landscape, cypress trees, olive groves — and the immersive community schedule create a naturally screen-minimal environment. About 1.5 hours from Pisa Airport by car.

Visit oshomiasto.it →

Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa (ILTK)

📍 Pomaia, near Pisa, Tuscany ☸️ Tibetan Buddhist (FPMT / Gelug) Duration: Weekend to multi-year residential
🚫 Phone-free during retreat periods

Founded in 1977 and part of FPMT (Foundation for the Preservation of the Mahayana Tradition), ILTK is one of the most respected Tibetan Buddhist study and practice centers in Europe. Located in Pomaia in the Tuscan hills south of Pisa, the center offers everything from beginner meditation introductions to multi-year residential study programs.

The institute hosts visiting teachers from the Tibetan Buddhist world and has a resident monastic community. Considered one of the most academically rigorous Buddhist institutes in Europe. About 1 hour from Pisa Airport by car.

Visit iltk.org →

Booking European retreats: Summer programs (June–September) at major European centers fill 3-4 months in advance. Plum Village's summer retreats open registration in January. Amaravati and Gaia House programs sell out weeks or months ahead. Get on mailing lists rather than checking websites occasionally.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the most famous digital detox retreat in Europe?

Plum Village in the Dordogne, France — founded by Thich Nhat Hanh in 1982 — is the most internationally recognized retreat center in Europe. Phones are not used during retreat periods, Noble Silence is observed, and practitioners from over 90 countries attend each year. It is one of the few places in the world where a truly phone-free culture is self-sustaining rather than enforced by policy.

Are European digital detox retreats in English?

Most major retreat centers welcoming international guests operate in English or offer English-language programs. All centers in England, Scotland, and Ireland are English-speaking. Plum Village (France) runs English retreats year-round. Suryalila (Spain) runs international English programs. Sakya Tashi Ling (Spain) offers programs in English, Catalan, and Spanish. Osho Miasto and ILTK (Italy) offer international programs in English. Always confirm when booking.

How much do European digital detox retreats cost?

Donation-based Buddhist centers (Amaravati in England) can be done for €200-400/week covering room and board. Mid-range centers like Gaia House run £90-150/night. Plum Village retreat weeks start around €500-700. Rigpa centers (Lerab Ling, Dzogchen Beara) are program-based, typically €400-1,000/week. Italy's ILTK starts around €100-200 for a weekend. Most centers offer bursaries or reduced pricing — always ask.

Which European country has the most retreat options?

England has the most concentrated retreat infrastructure: Amaravati (Hertfordshire), Gaia House (Devon), and Sharpham House (Devon) are all internationally respected, and Samye Ling (Scotland) is the oldest Tibetan Buddhist monastery in the Western world. France has Plum Village, probably the single most famous retreat center in Europe. Italy's Tuscany hosts two well-established meditation communities.
