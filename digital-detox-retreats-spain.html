Best Digital Detox Retreats in Spain (Phone-Free & Tech-Free)

Spain has been attracting holistic retreat-seekers for decades — particularly its Andalusian interior, where the Alpujarras mountain villages on the southern slopes of the Sierra Nevada have drawn alternative communities and wellness centers since the 1970s. The climate (long warm summers), the relative affordability, the rural beauty, and the distance from northern European frenzy have made southern Spain a natural home for retreat centers.

Cortijo Romero — in the Alpujarras near Granada — has been running English-language holistic retreats since 1986. Sakya Tashi Ling near Barcelona is a Tibetan Buddhist monastery. And Suryalila in Andalusia draws yoga practitioners from across Europe. All are genuinely phone-minimal in their retreat culture, even if formal policies vary.

Digital Detox Retreats in Spain

Cortijo Romero

Las Alpujarras, near Orgiva, Granada, Andalusia Screen-minimal culture English-language programs Operating since 1986 ~1 hour from Granada

Cortijo Romero is a holistic retreat center set in the Alpujarras mountains of Andalusia — the terraced villages and valleys on the southern face of the Sierra Nevada, at elevations between 700 and 1,500 meters. It has been running English-language holistic retreat programs since 1986, making it one of the longest-established retreat centers of its type in Europe.

The center is operated by a UK-based charitable organization and all programs are conducted in English. The weekly program roster includes yoga, meditation, authentic movement, creative arts, voice work, personal development workshops, and nature-based practices. The retreat culture is strongly screen-minimal — the combination of the setting, the program intensity, and the community culture means most guests naturally disconnect from devices during their stay.

The Alpujarras setting itself is remarkable — white-washed Moorish villages, chestnut and almond trees, terraced hillsides above deep river gorges, with the Sierra Nevada peaks (including the Mulhacén, the highest point on the Iberian peninsula at 3,479m) rising behind. This is not a flat yoga resort: it is a mountain landscape that enforces a kind of natural humility.

Getting there: Fly to Málaga (AGP) or Granada (GRX). Rent a car — the most practical option. From Málaga: approximately 1.5 hours east via A-44 to Lanjarón/Orgiva. From Granada: approximately 50-60 km south via A-44, about 1 hour. Some guests take a bus from Granada to Orgiva and arrange a taxi from there.

Cost: Week-long programs typically run £800-1,200 per person including shared accommodation and all meals. Check cortijo-romero.co.uk for current program listings.

Sakya Tashi Ling

Garraf Natural Park, Olivella, near Barcelona Phone-free during retreat programs Tibetan Buddhist (Sakya tradition) ~40 km from Barcelona

Sakya Tashi Ling is a Tibetan Buddhist monastery of the Sakya tradition, set within the Garraf Natural Park near Olivella in Barcelona province, approximately 40 km southwest of Barcelona city. It is one of the most significant Tibetan Buddhist monastery complexes in Spain, with a large ornate stupa and shrine hall in the style of Himalayan monastery architecture — a striking presence in the Mediterranean limestone landscape.

The center offers meditation courses, retreat programs, and philosophical teachings in the Tibetan Buddhist tradition, with programs available in Catalan, Spanish, and English. For digital detox purposes, formal retreat programs are conducted phone-free. Personal retreat accommodation and practice space is also available for those who want to use the monastery setting for independent retreat.

Its proximity to Barcelona makes it an accessible option for travelers who want to combine a city visit with a retreat period — a weekend or multi-day silent retreat at Sakya Tashi Ling is a 40-minute drive from El Prat airport.

Getting there: From Barcelona, drive via the C-32 coastal highway toward Sitges, then inland into the Garraf park (~40 km, about 45 minutes). Car rental from Barcelona airport is the most practical option. Some programs arrange shuttle transfers from Barcelona.

Suryalila Retreat Center

Cadiz province, Andalusia Screen-minimal yoga culture English-language programs Yoga teacher training & retreats

Suryalila is a yoga retreat center in the hills of Cadiz province in Andalusia, near the Sierra de Grazalema Natural Park — a dramatic landscape of white villages, limestone cliffs, and oak forests. It hosts yoga teacher training programs (the center is known especially for Jivamukti and alignment-based yoga trainings), wellness retreats, and yoga immersions, drawing participants primarily from the UK, Germany, the Netherlands, and the US.

All programs are conducted in English. The yoga retreat culture is strongly screen-minimal — intensive yoga programs are designed to be immersive, and the rural Cadiz setting (far from any city) naturally reinforces disconnection. While there is no hard phone ban across all programs, the culture and the schedule leave little space for or desire to be on devices.

The center has a pool, a beautiful yoga shala, and accommodation ranging from shared dorms to private rooms. The Andalusian hills around the center — hiking, olive groves, mountain villages — offer natural context for time away from screens.

Getting there: Fly into Seville (SVQ) or Jerez de la Frontera (XRY) and rent a car. Drive south/east through the Sierra de Grazalema area, approximately 1-2 hours from either airport depending on exact location. Check suryalila.com for current directions and transport options.

Visit suryalila.com →

Best time for a Spanish retreat: Spring (April–June) and autumn (September–October) are the best times for the Alpujarras and Andalusia — temperatures are comfortable (20-28°C), the landscape is green or in autumn color, and major retreat programs are in full swing. July–August is very hot in southern Spain (35°C+) and some centers reduce programming. Near Barcelona (Sakya Tashi Ling), the climate is milder and the center operates year-round.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best digital detox retreats in Spain?

The top digital detox retreats in Spain are Cortijo Romero (Alpujarras, near Orgiva/Granada — English-language holistic programs since 1986, screen-minimal culture), Sakya Tashi Ling (Garraf Natural Park near Barcelona — Tibetan Buddhist monastery, phone-free during programs), and Suryalila (Cadiz province, Andalusia — English-language yoga retreats, screen-minimal culture). Spain's Alpujarras region in Andalusia has the richest concentration of holistic retreat centers.

Are the retreats in Spain in English?

Cortijo Romero (Alpujarras) is run by a UK organization and all programs are in English. Suryalila (Cadiz) runs international English-language programs. Sakya Tashi Ling (Catalonia) offers programs in Catalan, Spanish, and English — confirm the language when booking. Most retreat centers targeting international visitors in Spain operate in English or offer bilingual programs.

How far is Cortijo Romero from Granada?

Cortijo Romero is approximately 60 km south of Granada city in the Alpujarras mountains — about 1 hour by car via the A-44 highway to Lanjarón and then the A-348 through Orgiva. Granada Airport (GRX) is approximately 70 km away. Many international guests fly into Málaga (AGP, ~120 km west) and drive east into the Alpujarras — about 1.5 hours.

What is Sakya Tashi Ling near Barcelona?

Sakya Tashi Ling is a Tibetan Buddhist monastery of the Sakya tradition in the Garraf Natural Park near Olivella, approximately 40 km southwest of Barcelona. It features a large ornate shrine hall and stupa, meditation courses, and personal retreat accommodation. Programs are available in Catalan, Spanish, and English. It is about 45 minutes from Barcelona El Prat Airport — one of the most accessible Buddhist retreat centers in Spain.
