Best Digital Detox Retreats in Europe (Phone-Free & Tech-Free)

Europe has some of the world's most respected contemplative retreat centers — many of them established before smartphones existed, in landscapes that have been places of pilgrimage and practice for centuries. From Thich Nhat Hanh's Plum Village monastery in the vineyards of Dordogne to a Tibetan Buddhist center perched on the Atlantic cliffs of County Cork, the European retreat landscape is both geographically varied and spiritually serious.

Unlike the American retreat market, which skews heavily toward wellness resort formats, European retreat centers tend to be rooted in specific traditions: Thai Forest Buddhism, Tibetan Buddhism, Zen, Vipassana, or long-standing holistic retreat lineages. The result is a phone-free culture that doesn't need to be enforced — it's embedded in how these places have always operated.

France

France is home to Plum Village, which is arguably the most famous retreat center in Europe and possibly the Western world. Founded by Thich Nhat Hanh in 1982 in the Dordogne countryside, it receives practitioners from over 90 countries every year. The south of France (Hérault, Languedoc) also houses Centre Lerab Ling, one of the major Tibetan Buddhist retreat centers in Europe. See all French digital detox retreats →

🇫🇷 France

Top centers: Plum Village (Thenac, Dordogne) — Thich Nhat Hanh's flagship monastery, Noble Silence, phones not used during retreat periods. Centre Lerab Ling (Hérault) — Rigpa's main European teaching center, Tibetan Buddhist, consecrated by the Dalai Lama.

Best for: Vipassana and mindfulness tradition; Tibetan Buddhist teachings; countryside immersion near Bordeaux wine country.

England & Scotland

England has the most established retreat infrastructure in Europe — multiple internationally recognized centers in Devon and Hertfordshire, plus Samye Ling in the Scottish borders. Devon in particular (Gaia House, Sharpham House) has become a regional hub for mindfulness-based retreat. See all UK digital detox retreats →

🇬🇧 England & Scotland

Top centers: Amaravati Buddhist Monastery (Hertfordshire) — Thai Forest tradition, phones collected at arrival. Gaia House (Devon) — Western vipassana, Noble Silence. Sharpham House (Devon) — mindfulness, Dart estuary setting. Samye Ling (Scotland) — largest Buddhist monastery in Western Europe.

Best for: First-time silent retreats (Gaia House beginner options); serious Theravada practice (Amaravati); Buddhist study and residential programs (Samye Ling).

Ireland

Ireland's retreat scene is anchored by Dzogchen Beara — a Tibetan Buddhist center on a clifftop on the Beara Peninsula in County Cork, with views of the Atlantic that make screens feel irrelevant. It is one of the most strikingly located retreat centers in Europe. See all Irish digital detox retreats →

🇮🇪 Ireland

Top center: Dzogchen Beara (Allihies, County Cork) — Tibetan Buddhist (Rigpa tradition), clifftop on the Atlantic, phones and devices not used during organized retreat programs. One of the most dramatically situated retreat centers in Europe.

Best for: Tibetan Buddhist practice; retreat combined with Irish West Coast scenery; accessible from Cork Airport.

Spain

Spain's retreat landscape is centered in the Alpujarras — the mountain villages on the southern slopes of the Sierra Nevada near Granada, which have attracted holistic retreat centers and alternative communities since the 1970s. Catalonia also has a significant Tibetan Buddhist monastery near Barcelona. See all Spanish digital detox retreats →

🇪🇸 Spain

Top centers: Cortijo Romero (Alpujarras, near Granada) — holistic retreat center in the Andalusian mountains, operating since 1986, programs in English. Sakya Tashi Ling (Garraf, Catalonia, near Barcelona) — Tibetan Buddhist monastery complex. Suryalila Retreat Center (Andalusia) — yoga retreat in the Cadiz hills.

Best for: Yoga and holistic retreat in warm climate; Tibetan Buddhist practice; Spanish countryside combined with meditation.

Italy

Italy's retreat scene is anchored in Tuscany, which hosts both an Osho meditation community and one of the most respected FPMT (Tibetan Buddhist) institutes in Europe. The Tuscan hills, olive groves, and relative remoteness from major cities reinforce the disconnection that these centers cultivate. See all Italian digital detox retreats →

🇮🇹 Italy

Top centers: Osho Miasto (Pomarance, near Volterra, Tuscany) — Osho meditation community in the Tuscan hills, international programs. Istituto Lama Tzong Khapa (Pomaia, near Pisa, Tuscany) — FPMT Tibetan Buddhist study and practice center, phones not used during retreat periods.

Best for: Osho meditation and bodywork; Tibetan Buddhist study; Tuscany setting with international programs in English.

Planning a European retreat: European retreat centers fill their summer programs (June–September) months in advance. For Buddhist centers like Plum Village and Amaravati, popular summer retreats can be fully booked 3-4 months out. Book early. Most centers have cancellation policies, but popular programs are genuinely limited in capacity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the best digital detox retreats in Europe?

Plum Village (France) is the most famous — Thich Nhat Hanh's monastery in the Dordogne, Noble Silence, truly phone-free during retreat periods, draws practitioners from 90+ countries. Amaravati (England) and Gaia House (England) are the most established silent retreat options in the UK. Dzogchen Beara (Ireland) has one of the most dramatic settings. Cortijo Romero (Spain) is the most accessible for holistic retreat beginners. See individual country pages for full listings.

Are European digital detox retreats in English?

Most major retreat centers that welcome international guests operate in English or offer English-language programs. Plum Village runs English retreats throughout the year. All UK and Irish centers are English-speaking. Cortijo Romero (Spain) is run by a UK organization with English programs. Osho Miasto (Italy) and Centre Lerab Ling (France) offer international programs in English. Always confirm the language when booking — some centers also offer programs in the local language.

How much do European retreat centers cost?

Donation-based Buddhist centers (Amaravati in England) can be done for €200-400/week. Mid-range holistic centers like Cortijo Romero or Sharpham run €100-250/night. Rigpa centers (Lerab Ling, Dzogchen Beara) are program-based, typically €400-1,000/week depending on program length. Plum Village retreat weeks run approximately €500-700 including accommodation and meals. Most centers offer reduced pricing — always ask about scholarships.

Which European country is best for a digital detox retreat?

England is the most accessible for English-speakers with the most variety — Amaravati (silent vipassana), Gaia House (insight meditation), Sharpham (mindfulness), Samye Ling (Tibetan Buddhist). France has Plum Village, the most internationally known. Ireland has Dzogchen Beara, the most dramatically situated. Spain's Alpujarras have the best climate for a longer retreat. Italy's Tuscany combines retreat depth with cultural richness.
