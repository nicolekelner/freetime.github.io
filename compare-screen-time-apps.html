Detailed App Reviews

Free Time

1 app free / $59.99/year

Free Time helps you build healthier phone habits with a clean, distraction-free approach. Block one app completely free, or upgrade to block unlimited apps and websites. Features usage analytics and scheduled sessions to help you stay on track.

Pros

  • 1 app blocking completely free
  • App and website blocking
  • Clean, simple interface
  • Detailed usage analytics
  • Scheduled blocking sessions

Cons

  • iOS only (no Android yet)
  • No desktop app

Opal

$99/year

Opal is one of the most popular screen time apps with a large user base. It offers comprehensive app and website blocking, detailed analytics, and social features. The app is beautifully designed with gamification elements like gems and streaks.

Pros

  • Beautiful, polished interface
  • Deep Focus mode is very effective
  • Available on iOS, Mac, and Android
  • Social features and community
  • Detailed weekly reports

Cons

  • Most expensive option at $99/year
  • Free version is very limited
  • Android features still catching up to iOS

Brick

$59 one-time

Brick takes a unique approach with a physical NFC device that you tap to unlock your blocked apps. Keep the Brick on your fridge, and you physically can't access distracting apps unless you walk over and tap it. No subscription required.

Pros

  • One-time payment, no subscription
  • Physical accountability is very effective
  • Works on iOS and Android
  • Multiple custom modes (Work, Family, etc.)
  • One device works with unlimited phones

Cons

  • Requires carrying/placing physical device
  • Can lose the physical Brick

One Sec

$19.99/year

One Sec takes a different approach: instead of blocking apps, it adds a brief pause and breathing exercise before you can open them. This "pattern interrupt" breaks the automatic habit of checking apps and has been shown to reduce social media use by 57%.

Pros

  • Most affordable option
  • Science-backed approach
  • Less restrictive than hard blocking
  • Helps build awareness of habits
  • Works on iOS and Android

Cons

  • Doesn't actually block apps
  • Relies on iOS Shortcuts (can be finicky)

Freedom

$39.99/year

Freedom is the only cross-platform solution that syncs blocking across all your devices - phone, tablet, and computer. Start a focus session on your Mac, and your iPhone is automatically blocked too. Great for people who need to block distractions everywhere.

Pros

  • Works on all platforms (iOS, Android, Mac, Windows, Chrome)
  • Synced blocking across devices
  • Website blocking included
  • Focus sounds and music
  • Lifetime plan available ($199)

Cons

  • Interface feels dated
  • Mobile app blocking less robust than desktop
  • Can be complex to set up

Clearspace

$59.99/year

Clearspace focuses on breaking the dopamine-driven impulse to open apps. It adds friction before accessing distracting apps, and has a unique feature where you can earn screen time by doing pushups or other activities.

Pros

  • Unique "earn screen time" features
  • Strict Mode is very effective
  • Social accountability with teammates
  • Works on iOS and Android
  • One app free

Cons

  • No website blocking
  • No desktop app

BePresent

$59.99/year

BePresent gamifies screen time reduction with leaderboards, points, and accountability partners. Make daily commitments and compete with friends to see who can stick to their goals best.

Pros

  • Gamification makes it engaging
  • Social accountability features
  • Leaderboards with friends
  • Daily commitment system

Cons

  • iOS only
  • Only 7-day free trial

Physical Device Apps

Bloom

$39 one-time

Bloom uses a physical NFC card to lock and unlock distracting apps. One purchase includes the card and lifetime app access - no subscription required. Features a child mode for parents who want to manage their kids' screen time.

Pros

  • Most affordable physical device option
  • No subscription required
  • Child Mode for parental controls
  • Website blocking included
  • Usage analytics and insights
  • Works on iOS and Android

Cons

  • Requires physical card to unlock
  • Can lose the card
  • Limited emergency exits

Unpluq

$26 tag + $64/year

Unpluq is an NFC tag that acts as a digital key to your blocked apps. The small rubberized tag comes with a carabiner and works offline - no charging or Bluetooth needed. However, it requires an annual subscription to use.

Pros

  • Compact, portable NFC tag
  • Works completely offline
  • No charging required
  • Family plan available
  • Works on iOS and Android

Cons

  • Requires annual subscription ($64/year)
  • Most expensive option over time
  • No website blocking
  • iOS limited to 49 apps
